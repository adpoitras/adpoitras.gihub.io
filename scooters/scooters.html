Electric Scooters

(top to bottom) Mark I, II, and III electric scooters

Brief Overview
  • I created three electric scooters, with each one building on the skills I gained from the last
  • The first one was a high school project where I attached a brushless motor system to a Razor kick scooter
  • The second one was an incremental upgrade from the first version, with the electronic parts from the first with a cnc aluminum body and a new battery
  • I used all the experience I gained building the first two to create the ultimate version
  • it features a fully custom chassis with front and rear pneumatic suspension, with power generated by a 4.4 kW motor and a larger custom made battery
  • Versions 2 and 3 features an information screen powered by an arduino, with the third being a full color LCD

Mark I: The First

Starting Out

When I first set out on creating my own electric scooter, I did not have much to work with. This was for a high school project, so I only had two months to do it. I had the experience with brushless motor control from building a quadcopter, though I did not have much knowledge in mechanical design. Nonetheless, I had a 3d printer and a few ideas.

I immediately decided to retrofit a Razor kick scooter with motors, as building a whole scooter was just simply beyond my scope at the time. I had to work with the very small chassis, so most of my parts were "tacked on". Initially I did not think there was enough space to fit a chain sprocket assembly in the back, so the motor was connected to a small drive wheel that was pressed against the main wheel with a spring. This didn't really work, as the wheel would just spin and settle on a groove. I then realized I could fit a small chain in there with a custom 3d printed sprocket that directly interfaced with the wheel spoke geometry.

Specifications

  • Motor: 3.5 kW brushless outrunner
  • speed controller: 60A VESC derivative
  • Battery: 5s 4200maH x2 in series
  • Wheels: 200 x 25 solid urethane
  • drivetrain: 3d printed sprockets, ~1.5 reduction
  • top speed: ~7 mph
  • max range: untested
  • Construction: 3D printed PLA
